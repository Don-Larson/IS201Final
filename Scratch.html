How to Make a Homemade Bow and Arrow

For 3 years my siblings and my mom made and sold homemade bows and arrows in order to pay for family trips.
Below is the process of making a bow and arrow as well as a video that we would display in our booth at craft fairs.

(The process of making the bow and arrows is not long, but it does require a few materials, tools, and prepwork before assembly can begin.)

  1. Materials
    • 3/4" PVC Pipe
    • Your favorite ductape
    • Scotch Tape
    • 1" plywood sheet (6" x 8" per handle)
    • 1" screws
    • Paracord
    • 1/4" wodden dowels
    • Pool noodles
    • Hotglue
    • paint
  2. Tools
    • Bandsaw
    • Mitersaw
    • Drill press
      • 1/8' drill bit
      • 1/4" drill bit
      • 3/8" drill bit
      • Step drill bit
    • Horizontal belt sander or horizontal radial sander
    • Screw gun
    • paint brush
    • Lighter
    • Scisors
    • PVC deburring tool
  3. Prep
    1. Take your PVC pipe and using the miter saw cut it into 3' pieces
    2. Using the bandsaw, cut the PVC pipe down the middle long ways(after doing this you should have 2 three foot PVC pieces). Because of the way that the band saw cuts, one piece will always be a bit thicker than the other.
    3. Take your favorite ductape apply it longways down the halved PVC pipe
    4. Using scotch tape, tape the ductape down along the innerside of the halved PVC pipe
    5. Using the 1/8" drill bit, take the thicker piece of PVC you set a side and drill a hole 1" from the top and the bottom centered on the PVC pipe.
    6. Still using th 1/8"drill bit, drill holes in the exact of the PVC half, as well as 4" towards the top and bottom from the center hole.
    7. Drill out the center hole with the 3/8" drill bit
    8. Set aside the thicker PVC side to be used later
    9. Print out the handle template found at madebykidsforkids.com and tape it down to the plywood. Cut out that shape using the bandsaw.
    10. Using the sander, shape it so it looks similar to the examples found on the madebykidsforkids.com website
    11. Paint the handle
    12. Using the drill press and the 3/8" drill bit, drill out the center of the handle as shown on madebykidsforkids.com
    13. Using the band or miter saw, cut the dowels into 18" pieces
    14. Cut pool noodles into 1" pieces using the band saw
    15. Take one blunt end of the dowel and using the band saw cut a line roughly 3/8" deep down the center of the dowel
    16. Using the 1/4" drill bit drill through the pool noodle like you are scewering a don't, but do not drill all the way through(only drill through a small portion of the second rung).
  4. Assembly
    1. Take the preped handle and newly ductaped PVC half, line them up so that the center holes of both match
    2. Take the drill gun and the screws and screw through the holes 4" away from the center into the wooden handle.
    3. Take paracord and using the holes 1" from the top, tie the string to the PVC.
    4. Cut the paracord about 3 inches longer than the lenth of the bent pipe end to end.
    5. Stick the paracord through the other hole and tie the paracord while bending the pvc pipe a bit.
    6. Burn the cut ends of the paracord to prevent fraying
    7. Take the cone drill bit and drill out a bit of the center hold on both the front and handle side of the bow so that a dowel can slide in and out without catching
    8. Take a dowel and stick it thorugh the holes you made in one of the pool noodle pieces making sure that the knotched site of the dowel is facing out.
    9. Hot glue the dowel to the pool noodle piece
